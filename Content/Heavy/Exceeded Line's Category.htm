Exceeded Line's Category

IM assigns line categories to their lines. RU must verify that all wagons in the train composition are capable and suitable of running on the forwarding route's line category, in respect of axle load and meter load.
For simplifying the handling of the check:

  • Rail wagons have determined payload limits in classes that can be found in the load tables displayed on their sides.
  • Rail vehicles have assigned line category they comply with.

Definition

Loaded vehicles and freight wagons become heavy load consignments when they exceed the forwarding route's line category.

Vertical quasi-static load

European infrastructure managers use provisions from EN 15528 to classify their lines, considering parameters like:

  • Axle load.
  • Mass per unit length.
  • Geometrical aspects relating to the spacing of axles.
  • Speed.

In this manner IMs provide method for determining compatibility of a vehicle's vertical load characteristics with load carrying capacity of lines on the network. The axle load and axle spacing of the vehicles define the vertical quasi-static load input to the track. IMs allocate categories of lines, or of section of lines, in the categories from A to D4 with the specified maximum axle load, and the maximum permissible load per unit length. Some IMs, also approve classes E4, and E5 for freight wagons on lines with tracks designed for heavier loads.

Linear load

Linear load calculate with the formula: 

Calculating the weight of the load remember to include the weight securing devices (as appropriate). Apply wagon length measured over the uncompressed buffers.

Static axle load

Static axle load calculate with the formula: 

Calculating the weight of the load remember to include the weight of securing devices (as appropriate).

This simple formula used in rail transport has worked well in the past for relatively simple wagon designs and unified loading methods. The axle load of a uniformly loaded two- or four-axle wagons can be roughly estimated with this calculation formula. This calculation result is an estimate, as it does not take into account the geometrical characteristics of the wagons and vehicles. In the case of the vehicles with two 3-axle bogies, or vehicles build with irregular distribution of weight between the individual axles, this formula cannot be used to calculate the axle load. In such cases, quasi-static calculations must be used and relied upon.

Information sources on tracks load category

National RUs for many years have published inventories, with line classes available on commercially offered routes in their countries. These list used to be published on the UIC LOCA Load Capacity website. Due to a lack of regular provision of updated lists by some of RUs, in the year 2023 UIC decided in voting, to remove the resource from the website, and to archive its contents.

At present, information on the applicable classes on the lines of the managers should be obtained from the network managers, for example from:

  • IM's network statement.
  • IM's published network maps of load capacity.
  • Technical Standards of IM's on upgrading or constructing railway lines.
  • IM's IT systems for ordering paths.

Caution: In some networks or just on selected lines or sidings the maximum load may be lower. Always inspect the permissible maximum load on the entire planned route. Poland is one of the countries where just under 20% of paths have the maximum load capacity of 20.0 tonnes, and where an additional load of 0.5 t per wheelset in class C requires an exceptional consignment authorisation.
