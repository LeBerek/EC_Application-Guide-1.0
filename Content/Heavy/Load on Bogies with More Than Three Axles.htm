Load on Bogies With More Than Three Axles

In line with IRS 50502, loaded rail vehicles with more than three axles per bogie are forwarded as exceptional consignments. On some networks, however, the special construction wagons with over eight axles are exceptional consignments irrespective of whether they are loaded or not.

So called Schnabel wagons series Uaa...are special construction wagons, that are used for moving dimensional and/or heavy types of goods. Goods in such wagons are loaded in one of the three manners, as load:

  • Rested on a low loading wagon bridge.
  • Self–supported between the two ends of the wagon by lifting arms, and becomes part of the wagon.
  • Suspended on the through–loading bridge (resting on the side boards).

Lowered platforms – have a lowered loading level (loading bridge).
These platforms are designed to transport goods with great heights.

Schnabel wagons with forward boards supporting the load unit are designed for the carriage of consignments with large external dimensions and high unit weights. These wagons consists of two symmetrical halves ("half-wagons"), which are connected to each other by means of elements used for securing the consignment to be carried. The cargo placed on the headboards of these wagon halves becomes, in a way, part of the wagon for the duration of transport.

Schnabel wagons with side boards - they have load-bearing sides in the form of longitudinal beams that enable shipments to be carried in a suspended position. These wagons are designed to carry shipments with great heights and high unit weights.

Schnabel wagons with forward boards and sideboards are often equipped with additional devices for extending the load arms, depending on the width of the consignments to be transported.

These wagons are also often equipped with additional devices to move the horizontal, lateral and vertical load-bearing bridge or frame in order to allow the consignment to be transported next to or under the critical objects.

Empty Schnabel wagons run with the two lifting arms connected to one another (short coupled), often running at normal freight train speeds. Due to their design these wagons may also exceed the gauge when they run in the empty condition. Another reason for your concern should be wagon interoperability status. These wagons usually have no interoperability.
