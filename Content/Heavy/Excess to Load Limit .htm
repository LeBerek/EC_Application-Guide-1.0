Excess to Load Limit within Carrying Capacity

Definition

Loaded wagons are considered "heavy load consignment" when they are loaded in excess of the maximum load limit marked on them (load limit label / additional load limit label) up to the maximum design carrying capacity of a wagon (circle symbol).

Use Cases

The case of excess load to load limit with carrying capacity applies, for example, to 6-axle flat wagons on the two 3-axle bogies.

Load Limits Check – Example

A customer asked if he could get an authorisation and conditions for the carriage of a load of slabs, weighing in total 101 t, on the wagon designed for transport of that type of goods. You look at parameters of the 6-axle, Samms 469 wagon owned by DB Cargo DE. Client intends to transport goods from Germany to Poland.

Wagon geometrical characteristics

Wagon tare weight

Wagon maximum design carrying capacity

Load mark in circle informs you that wagon could be suitable for the load the client asked for. Maximum design carrying capacity is higher than the load's weight. This parameter, however, does not inform you about the line class on the intended route which you could apply for, on the networks in Germany and Poland.

Load limit table

Load limit table contains load limits that are calculated quasi-statically in accordance with EN 15528. According to these rules, the load you are inspecting, cannot be transported in any class indicated in the load limit table, because your load weighs 101 tonnes and the highest load limit in class D is 75.5 tonnes.

Concentrated loads table

In case of concentrated loads, a reduction of the payload is applied, and it depends on the good's length and the way of bedding of the concentrated load. For the load in question, you compare information about the loading method provided by the customer with values of the permitted concentrated loads on the wagon. In this case client wants to load slabs on two base plates with the distance of 9 meters between the two supports. The load will be symmetrically distributed on the wagon.

The result of the verification carried out is positive.
The proposed load in question:

  • Falls within the maximum design carrying capacity of the wagon.
  • Fulfils the conditions specified for concentrated loads rested on two supports 9 meters apart.
  • In Germany does not qualify for carriage within the rail line classes limits.

  • In Poland does not qualify for carriage within the rail line classes limits.

The load and the wagon you just inspected are eligible for approval procedure under the rules for heavy goods consignments which are loaded in excess of the maximum load limit marked on them (load limit label / additional load limit label) up to the maximum design carrying capacity of a wagon (circle symbol).
