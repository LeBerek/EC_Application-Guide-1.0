Maximum Construction Gauge for Rolling Stock

Maximum construction gauge for rolling stock is one of the technical characteristics of a railway vehicle and concerns the permitted size of a vehicle. More precisely, the maximum construction gauge for rolling stock is the result of a calculation, based on a reference profile and its associated calculation rules, and defines the vehicle’s maximum exterior dimensions to prevent contact by the vehicle with the infrastructure.

Rolling stock constructors apply one of the three calculation methods. A vehicle's conformity with a gauge is declared based on a calculation method referred to as:

  • Static.
  • Kinematic.
  • Dynamic.

The calculation methods within a gauge, for example G1, are independent. That is, depending on the calculation method used, the vehicle design will make more or less use of the railway lines' room capacity provided by the infrastructure manager.

The static method is the oldest calculation method, and the most conservative. At the same time, it is the simplest to apply. Also, the method that RUs apply for the evaluation of consignment dimensions, so they can determine whether they constitute an exceptional consignment, is based on this method.

Since 2009, wagons have been marked with the kinematic gauge to which they were built. Since 2014, the gauge marking has been updated and additionally includes a separate marking of the lower and upper gauges.

There are different gauges defined since years:

  • G with the numeral 1 or 2:
    • G1 - is the smallest gauge and most interoperable one.
    • G2 - is bigger in the upper parts.
    • These gauges were the only ones defined in the old UIC Leaflet 505-series.
  • G with the letter A, B or C for combined transport - for example GC:
    • These 3 gauges were developed for loads.
  • G with additional letter I and with the numeral 1, 2 or 3 for the lower than 400 mm from the rail head part of the vehicle - for example: GI2

G1, GA, GB and GC for the upper parts, and GI1 and GI2 are the only interoperable gauges defined in TSI INF.

Only the vehicle gauge G1/GI1 is a truly international vehicle gauge (except for Great Britain). Other vehicle gauges might require additional checks.

Before operating authorised vehicles, the RU should conduct a Route compatibility check As described in Appendix D1 of TSI OPE (Commission Implementing Regulation (EU) 2019/773). in line with its Safety Management System SMS Safety Management System. Note: Interoperable, Multinational and National gauges do not currently contain data. These parameters are left for informational reasons only.

Vehicle markings G1, G2 on wagons and other vehicles refer to the kinematic gauges as specified in TSI Technical Specifications for Interoperabilitys.

Warning: Some IMs, for example in Poland, require all vehicles brought onto their network by an RU to conform with the static vehicle gauge profiles.

For IMs requiring conformity with the static vehicle gauge, just comparing the ERATV data and RINF data may be insufficient to decide whether a vehicle built to kinematic gauge actually conforms with the static gauge provisions and is compatible with the static vehicle gauge profile. In such a case the only way to examine the vehicle's size is to run an inspection of a detailed vehicle drawing or a study with calculations of the vehicle's restriction analysis. The later type of document is rarely made available to RUs.
