Structure Gauge

Railway undertakings are responsible for determining and maintaining the correct rolling stock gauge during transport on a network. One of the most frequently asked questions in rail transport is, what is the largest train (consisting vehicles, or loaded wagons) that the railway network being considered can accommodate? The answer depends on the network's capacity to accept trains larger than those internationally interoperable without restrictions.

Infrastructure Gauging Methods

Three infrastructure gauging methods are currently applied in Europe:

  • Defined gauging
  • Absolute gauging
  • Comparative gauging

Europe has generally adopted a standard UIC approach of defined gauging where infrastructure gauges are defined based on the reference profile and application of associated rules. This method is originally defined in the 505 series of leaflets and is nowadays, described in the series of European Standard EN 15273.

EN 15273 Norm – Defined Gauge Method

Defined gauging is a convention between the infrastructure manager and the rolling stock, where:

  1. The infrastructure manager needs a gauge to know how to maintain and develop their network for safe and efficient operations.
  2. The rolling stock needs to know gauge to safely move loaded wagons or to run powered vehicles, or coaches on their own wheels.

The defined gauging methodology ensures that there is never any contact between vehicle and structure.

Calculation methods

The defined gauging method describes in the norm is split into three main calculation methods:

  1. The static method uses fixed values and is interoperable until the limit values of the flexibility coefficient.
  2. The kinematic method is the most interoperable calculation method in Europe.
  3. The dynamic method is used on certain networks with the aim of optimizing the space available by using computer calculations by the rolling stock.

Information: Infrastructure gauges should not be compared solely by their profiles since the rules associated which each profile differ from each other.

Infrastructure Gauge Types

Four types of structure gauges are defined:

  1. Infrastructure Verification Limit Gauge - defines the space not to be encroached upon at any time. It fixes the limit for normal operation. It is used to ensure that structures allow free passage. Consequently, no structure is allowed to penetrate this space at any time.
  2. Infrastructure Installation Limit Gauge - gives the space to be cleared taking into account a maintenance allowance. The allowance is defined according to the line speed and the track quality at the time of the structure installation. The maintenance allowances have defined maintenance levels which are examined to ensure that the minimum clearance shall always be maintained and allow the safe operation of the vehicles.
  3. Infrastructure Installation Nominal Gauge - in addition to maintenance allowances, the structure installation nominal gauge takes into account the safety allowances and additional reserved allowances defined for the infrastructure, for example for the running of special consignments, for line speed increase, strong cross winds or aerodynamic effects, and so on.
  4. Uniform Structure Gauge - corresponds to a unique contour, evaluated by the infrastructure manager's diagnostics; it includes the most unfavourable situations with significant safety margins.

TSI define one gauging method – the kinematic gauging. New lines should adhere to infrastructure installation nominal guage, however the infrastructure manager has the option to adopt a uniform structure gauge.

Information: In Poland, the infrastructure manager has established two uniformed structure gauges. The GPL-1 gauge is used when upgrading or constructing new railway lines or sections of lines for speeds of 200 km/h or less. On the other hand, the GPL-2 gauge is used for modernizing or constructing new railway lines or sections of lines for speeds greater than 200 km/h, and for all new civil engineering structures and platform shelters, regardless of speed. The uniformed structure gauges are to be assessed separately for the section up to a height of 1,170 mm and for the section above it.

Structure Gauge Constrictions

Constriction may be classified into 4 groups

Group 1

Restrictions in the overall space (sideways and upwards)

  • Tunnel tubes
  • Arched overpasses
  • Bridges with wind reinforcement at the top
  • Electrical overhead contact lines

Group 2

Width restrictions

  • Retaining walls
  • Bridge railing
  • Masts, Signals

Group 3

Width and height restrictions

  • Overpasses
  • Platform roofs

Group 4

Height and width restrictions in the area of 0 to X mmm above the top of rail:

  • Platform edges
  • Goods platforms
  • Track switches
  • Dwarf signals
  • Bridge reinforcements

  • Other low located elements of infrastructure

Note: Infrastructure managers determine individually on their networks how high above the rail head zone 4 ends. The value of X can therefore take on different values and is not specified here.
